Common questions

A checkout report documents the condition of your rental property when you move out. It provides timestamped, evidence-backed proof of how you left the property — which is crucial if your landlord or agent disputes your bond claim. Without one, it's your word against theirs.

Fair wear and tear is the natural deterioration that happens from normal, everyday use — things like minor scuffs on walls, faded curtains, or light carpet wear in high-traffic areas. Damage, on the other hand, goes beyond normal use — like holes in walls, burns, or broken fixtures. Your landlord cannot claim your bond for fair wear and tear under NSW and VIC tenancy law.

Right now, we're focused on NSW and VIC because tenancy law varies by state. Our reports are tailored to the Residential Tenancies Acts in those two states. We're planning to expand to Queensland and other states soon — register your interest and we'll let you know when we launch in your area.

If your landlord or agent refuses to release your bond even after receiving your checkout report, our Bond Advocate plan includes direct negotiation on your behalf. If it still can't be resolved, we'll help you prepare for a tribunal hearing at NCAT (NSW) or VCAT (VIC) with all the documentation you need. Most disputes are resolved well before that stage.
